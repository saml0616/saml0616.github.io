Hey there! Want to get to know me?

UX is not just about designing great experiences, it is about creating great experiences with services that help users solve their problems. Having a background in computer science helps me when trying to brainstorm innovative solutions. It provides me an understanding of the limitations and opportunities that available technologies offer. As a result, the products I design takes advantage technology in ways which aims to streamlines the user’s life.

In addition, as a designer with a background in computer science, I am able to understand the needs and priorities of both the design team and the engineering team. Therefore, I can design services that does not sacrifice the user’s experience for technical implementation. In addition, these skills allow me to communicate efficiently with both designs and also software engineers.

My UX Background

I was introduced to the topic of “User Experience” when I attended a coding bootcamp during the Summer between my Sophomore and Junior year of college. I wasn’t able to do much regarding UX during my time there but it was definitely a field that I was very much interested in. I didn’t attend a design school, so when my Junior year started, I took it upon myself to learn about UX and create designs for any idea I had that could help users. While the designs I made during that time were not the greatest, it provided me the opportunity to be a UX Intern at a company called Mobile Heartbeat. My time at Mobile Heartbeat taught me a lot about common UX practices and the importance of having a design process. With the skills I learnt on my own and during my internship, I have designed services with the UX process from ideation to user testing.
